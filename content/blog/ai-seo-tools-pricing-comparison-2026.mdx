AI SEO pricing is difficult to compare because the number shown on a pricing card rarely represents the full cost.

One vendor displays a monthly price that is only available when the full year is paid upfront. Another charges per user. Another includes a limited number of prompts, documents, crawl credits, or tracked keywords and then sells additional capacity. Some products do not publish paid prices at all.

This guide compares the pricing models and public entry points that matter when choosing an SEO suite, content optimizer, crawler, AI assistant, or execution workflow.

## Pricing verification and disclosure

> **Last verified: July 17, 2026.** Prices, plan names, limits, promotions, taxes, and regional currency conversions can change. Confirm the final checkout amount and contract terms on each vendor's official pricing page.

This comparison follows four rules:

1. **Monthly billing and annual billing are shown separately.** A price described as “per month, billed annually” is a monthly equivalent, not a month-to-month subscription.
2. **Annual licences are not converted into pretend monthly plans.** Screaming Frog, for example, sells an annual per-user licence.
3. **Add-ons and usage limits are part of the price.** Seats, prompts, crawl credits, tracked pages, documents, API credits, and overages can materially change the bill.
4. **Non-public pricing is labelled as non-public.** We do not estimate a paid price when the vendor asks buyers to book a demo.

All prices below are in USD unless another currency is explicitly shown.

## What the headline price usually leaves out

### 1. Annual prepayment

A vendor may display `$99/month` while requiring the customer to pay the full year upfront. The monthly number is useful for comparison, but cash flow and cancellation flexibility are different from a true monthly plan.

Check for wording such as:

- billed annually
- annual commitment required
- paid upfront
- save 17% or 20% annually

### 2. Seats and users

Some tools include one user and charge for each additional person. Others include unlimited users but meter documents, pages, prompts, or credits instead.

A team of five should compare:

`base plan + required seats + collaboration add-ons`

—not only the single-user entry price.

### 3. Tracked keywords, prompts, pages, and documents

The relevant unit depends on the product:

- SEO suites meter projects, tracked keywords, crawl credits, exports, and API units.
- AI-visibility tools meter prompts, platforms, locations, domains, or checks.
- Content tools meter documents, drafts, pages, audits, or tracked topics.
- Crawlers may meter users rather than websites.
- Execution systems may meter connected sites and approved actions.

Before buying, estimate the volume you will use every month and compare it with the included capacity.

### 4. Add-ons and overages

Examples of costs that may sit outside the base plan include:

- additional users
- AI prompt packages
- API access or credits
- white-label reporting
- local-search locations
- extra tracked keywords
- extra pages or drafts
- pay-as-you-go content or model usage
- onboarding, migration, or enterprise support

Do not assume an advertised AI feature is included at the capacity your team needs.

### 5. Taxes and currency conversion

Taxes, VAT, GST, card conversion, and regional pricing can change the final invoice. Screaming Frog, for example, publishes a GBP base price and also supports USD and EUR pricing. Compare the actual checkout currency your company will use.

## Pricing details by tool category

### Broad SEO suites: Semrush, Ahrefs, and SE Ranking

Broad suites are usually the highest-value purchase when the team needs several of these in one account:

- competitor and keyword research
- rank tracking
- backlink data
- site auditing
- historical data
- reporting
- API or export access
- AI-search visibility monitoring

The cost becomes inefficient when the team uses only one narrow feature or already has equivalent data elsewhere.

**Pricing decision:** compare the plan that contains the required limits, not the vendor's lowest promotional or starter tier.

### Content and AI-search optimization: Surfer, Clearscope, and Frase

These tools are easier to justify when writers and editors repeatedly create or refresh pages.

The most important cost units are:

- documents or articles per month
- pages monitored or audited
- tracked AI prompts
- domains and brand workspaces
- seats
- automated publishing or agent capacity

A plan can look inexpensive until the editorial calendar exceeds its document or audit limits.

### Technical crawling: Screaming Frog

Screaming Frog has a different model from most SaaS subscriptions:

- the free version crawls up to 500 URLs per crawl
- the paid licence is annual
- each user needs a licence
- bulk discounts start at five users

That makes it inexpensive for one technical SEO operator but requires a separate calculation for a larger team.

### Content strategy: MarketMuse

MarketMuse publishes the structure and limits of its Free, Optimize, Research, and Strategy plans, but does not display paid amounts publicly. The honest comparison is therefore:

- Free: public, limited to 10 queries per month
- Paid plans: contact/demo pricing

A comparison article should not insert an old third-party MarketMuse price as if it were current.

### General AI assistants: ChatGPT

ChatGPT can support research, synthesis, clustering, drafting, and analysis. It does not include the same native keyword, backlink, crawl, or verified Search Console datasets as an SEO platform.

For a team comparison, ChatGPT Business is currently shown at:

- $25 per user per month on monthly billing
- $20 per user per month when billed annually

Its cost should be treated as an AI workspace layer, not a replacement for every SEO data source.

## How to compare total cost of ownership

Use this formula:

`annual base cost + seats + add-ons + expected overages + implementation time + service cost`

Then measure the result against:

- hours of manual analysis removed
- number of actions shipped
- time from finding to deployment
- pages improved
- errors avoided through governance
- measurable click, ranking, crawl, conversion, or AI-visibility outcomes

A tool with a higher subscription can be cheaper if it replaces several overlapping products or significantly reduces implementation time. A cheap tool can be expensive if its output becomes another report no one acts on.

## Questions to ask before purchasing

1. Is the displayed monthly price available month to month, or only with annual prepayment?
2. Which user, project, domain, keyword, prompt, page, document, and crawl limits apply?
3. What happens at the limit: work stops, the plan upgrades, or overage charges begin?
4. Are AI visibility, API access, exports, white labelling, and additional users included?
5. Is the free trial automatic, and does it require a card?
6. Can the plan be cancelled immediately, or only at the next renewal?
7. Is the feature generally available, beta, an add-on, or restricted to enterprise?
8. What other tools or people are still required to turn the output into a shipped change?

## FAQ

### What is the cheapest AI SEO tool?

Google Search Console is free for verified properties, and several vendors offer free or limited entry tiers. The cheapest useful option depends on the required job. A free first-party data source does not provide competitor backlinks, content optimization, or execution by itself.

### What does “per month, billed annually” mean?

It is the monthly equivalent of an annual commitment. The customer normally pays the full annual amount upfront rather than paying that amount one month at a time. Always check the checkout total and cancellation terms.

### Which AI SEO tools charge add-ons?

Many do. Common add-ons include users, AI prompt capacity, API credits, reports, white labelling, tracked keywords, local-search locations, pages, drafts, and content usage. The base plan should never be evaluated without the required add-ons.

### Are the SERP Strategists paid plans available now?

The free Growth Audit is available now. The $49 Growth and $99 Scale prices are early-access targets for approved accounts, not active self-serve subscriptions. Joining early access does not start billing, and final scope and limits are confirmed before activation.

### How often should a pricing comparison be updated?

Review volatile pricing pages at least quarterly and before any purchase recommendation. Update immediately when a vendor changes plan names, billing structure, included limits, or add-on pricing.

## Final takeaway

Do not compare AI SEO tools using one number copied from the top of each pricing page.

Compare:

- the correct plan for your required workflow
- monthly versus annual commitment
- users and usage limits
- add-ons and overages
- the cost of the tools or people still needed after the recommendation is generated

The best-priced stack is the smallest combination that produces reliable evidence and consistently turns it into measurable work.
