## What is Generative Engine Optimization?

Generative Engine Optimization is the practice of structuring and improving content so AI-powered search engines can cite, summarize, and trust it in generated answers.

The short version:

- traditional SEO helps you rank in search results
- Generative Engine Optimization helps you get cited in AI-generated answers
- most brands now need both

If someone asks what GEO means, that is the definition they need first.

## GEO vs SEO: What's the Difference?

| | Traditional SEO | GEO |
|---|---|---|
| **Target** | Google SERP rankings | AI-generated answers |
| **Goal** | Get clicked | Get cited |
| **Signals** | Backlinks, keywords, page speed | Authority, structure, clarity |
| **Format** | Any web page | Structured, factual content |
| **Measurement** | Position, CTR | Citations, brand mentions |

**SEO** gets you ranked on Google. **Generative Engine Optimization** gets you cited by AI.

You need both.

## What makes content more citable in AI search?

AI models tend to cite content that is:

### 1. Structured with schema and clear page meaning

AI engines heavily favor content with clear JSON-LD schema. This tells the model exactly what your content is about:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "HowTo",
  "name": "How to optimize for GEO",
  "step": [...]
}
```

**Action:** Add Article, FAQ, HowTo, and Organization schema to every page.

### 2. Clear about who wrote it and what the site knows

AI needs to understand WHO you are and WHAT you're an authority on. This means:

- Clear author bios with credentials
- Consistent brand mentions across the web
- Topic clustering (write 5-10 posts on one topic, not 1 post on 10 topics)

### 3. Specific, factual, and easy to quote

AI models love content that contains clear, definitive statements they can quote:

> **Bad:** "SEO is kind of important for businesses that want to grow online."

> **Good:** "SEO drives 53% of all website traffic, making it the single largest source of organic acquisition for B2B companies."

### 4. Organized around direct questions and answers

FAQ sections are gold for GEO. AI models frequently pull answers directly from FAQ markup:

```html
<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [{
    "@type": "Question",
    "name": "What is GEO?",
    "acceptedAnswer": {
      "@type": "Answer",
      "text": "GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is the practice of optimizing content to be cited by AI search engines."
    }
  }]
}
</script>
```

### 5. Recently updated and maintained

AI models prefer recently updated content. Adding a "Last updated" date and regularly refreshing content signals relevance.

## The simplest GEO checklist

1. Add schema markup to important pages.
2. Write clear, factual statements that stand on their own.
3. Use FAQ sections where direct answers help.
4. Build topic clusters instead of isolated pages.
5. Add statistics, sources, and expert attribution.
6. Keep important pages fresh.
7. Check whether AI platforms are actually citing you.
