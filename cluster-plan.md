## Strategic decision

Replace the narrow SERP-analysis pillar with:

> **AI SEO Automation: Practical Workflows, Tutorials, and Results**  
> `/blog/ai-seo-automation-guide`

The site should not compete as another generic “AI writes SEO content” blog. It should teach how AI-assisted SEO systems actually work: real data inputs, repeatable steps, human approval points, failure modes, and measurable outputs.

Current search results support this structure. Broad automation results are complete workflow guides, while AI-audit results are dominated by audit products and AI-content-workflow results are dedicated editorial tutorials. They should be separate spokes, not one overloaded article. Representative sources include [EasyClaw’s automation guide](https://easyclaw.com/blog/knowledge/seo-automation-complete-guide/), [Frase’s agentic SEO guide](https://www.frase.io/blog/ai-agents-for-seo), and [ClusterMagic’s human-AI content workflow](https://www.clustermagic.ai/blog/ai-seo-content-workflow).

## Authority model

Every new article must contain:

- the real input dataset or a sanitized sample;
- exact tools, prompts, API calls, or automation steps;
- screenshots, code, templates, or downloadable artifacts where useful;
- explicit human review and approval gates;
- what failed, what required manual correction, and what should not be automated;
- before-and-after metrics from GSC, GA4, crawl data, rankings, citations, or conversions;
- author and reviewer credentials, methodology, `dateModified`, and a visible changelog;
- current primary-source citations for platform and policy claims.

This is the core authority strategy. Publishing more AI-written pages without proof would weaken the positioning.

## Tutorial format

Use the same structure for automation tutorials:

1. Problem and expected outcome.
2. Inputs and prerequisites.
3. Workflow diagram.
4. Exact implementation steps.
5. Quality gates and rollback.
6. Example output.
7. Measurement after 7, 28, and 90 days.
8. Failure modes and limitations.
9. Reusable checklist, prompt, code, or template.

The current SERP for AI content workflows consistently emphasizes human review and quality control, including [SEO-True’s supervised workflow](https://seo-true.com/blog/automate-your-seo-content-with-ai-complete-workflow-guide) and [GoSEO’s verification guidance](https://www.goseo.to/help/features/ai-content-generator).

## Cannibalization rules

- The pillar owns the broad “AI SEO automation guide” intent.
- “What is AI SEO?” stays conceptual and sends implementation traffic to the pillar.
- The content workflow covers research-to-publish operations; the internal-linking and keyword-research tutorials stay task-specific.
- The AI technical audit page covers crawl-to-fix implementation, not tool rankings.
- The existing best-tools page remains commercial; tutorials should mention tools only in the context of a reproducible workflow.
- GEO, AI visibility measurement, and E-E-A-T remain distinct: optimization, measurement, and trust evidence respectively.
- Do not publish standalone pages for navigational queries such as product logins.

## Internal linking

- Every spoke links to the pillar and the pillar links to every spoke.
- Every three-page cluster is fully cross-linked.
- The four-page authority cluster uses a bidirectional ring so every page still receives at least three incoming links including the pillar.
- The exact 64-link adjacency list and anchor text are stored in `cluster-plan.json`.
- Validation target: zero orphan pages and no repeated primary keyword.

## Publishing order

1. Publish the AI SEO automation pillar.
2. Publish “What Is AI SEO?” and connect the existing foundation pages.
3. Publish the keyword-research, content-workflow, and internal-linking tutorials.
4. Publish technical audit, GSC automation, and schema automation tutorials.
5. Refresh the existing tools and AI-search pages so each demonstrates its relationship to the pillar.
6. Publish case studies only after real experiments have complete baseline and post-change data.

## Measurement

- Organic: clicks, impressions, CTR, average position, and non-brand query growth.
- Authority: earned links, branded searches, expert mentions, and citations in AI answers.
- Tutorial quality: template downloads, code/template reuse, scroll depth, and return visits.
- Product intent: assisted visits to product, pricing, or contact pages.
- Accuracy: corrections, stale claims, failed workflows, and validation pass rate.
- Review cadence: update tutorials quarterly or whenever a named platform/API changes.

## Limitations

- Monthly search volume and difficulty data were unavailable; no volume estimates were invented.
- WebSearch was used instead of a location-locked DataForSEO export.
- This update changes planning artifacts only. It does not create, publish, or deploy articles.
